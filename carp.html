This poster has heavy contrast between the colour scheme of black, light red and white. The typography in this poster is mostly dilatational, extending from a point of focus from the logo at the centre of the page. I intentionally cut the edges of the "True" and "False" text off the page. I'm pretty sure this has an actual term in graphic design and I remember hearing something about it during a workshop, but I honestly forgot. I think I could have pushed the text a bit more off the canvas and it might have looked better.

This is my first poster, which features pobably the most common idea being where the page is divided in two at an angle by the True/False logo. This design went through a few iterations because my first few attempts seemed to me a bit bland. Most of the text is aligned to the right side in the red area.

For this poster I have a different colour scheme because the red/white/black scheme for the rest of the posters was getting boring, and I wanted to use a colour scheme which would conform with the rest of my website. This builds on the theme of repetition as the grey background contains repeated use of check marks, crosses and "TRUEFALSE" text, each in different segments. I decided to use a more Dilatational approach to the typography in this poster.

This was my last poster where I decided to place the majority of the text in close proximity in the bottom left corner, I experimented a bit with polygons in this piece, the close proximity of the text allowed me to make use of a lot of free space to make some interesting geometry. It also kind of came together to be in the "Z" pattern which catches the eye movement of the viewer.

     

These are my iterations (for my first poster anyway) All posters had an original version without borders- it shouldn't be too hard to imagine. I added them because it helped make each poster look more "full" to me.
